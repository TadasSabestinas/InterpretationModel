About this tool

A coverage report interpretation tool developed as part of a bachelor's thesis on extending JaCoCo's standard metrics with derived measures, augmented by an LLM interpretation layer.

What it does

  • Single-report analysis: upload a JaCoCo XML and get a full breakdown: composite quality score and grade (A–F), all derived metrics at project, package, class, and method level. Click any package or class to drill in.
  • Hotspot detection: four risk categories (coverage gap risk, branch blind spots, shallow tests, untested complexity) automatically flag the classes most likely to harbour untested behaviour, filtered to classes with cyclomatic complexity ≥ 3.
  • Quality distribution: histogram showing how classes spread across quality score buckets, so you can see whether low scores are isolated outliers or a systemic pattern.
  • Side-by-side comparison: upload two JaCoCo XMLs to compare metrics between projects, or before and after a refactor, with per-metric delta highlighting.
  • AI interpretation: any metric view can be sent to an LLM for a natural-language reading. The exact prompt is always inspectable via the "Show prompt" toggle.

The metrics

Mean(line, branch), used in quality score

(line% + branch%) / 2

Arithmetic mean of JaCoCo's line and branch coverage percentages. Surfaces the common pathology of high line coverage paired with low branch coverage in a single number. Classes with no branch counter are treated as 100% branch coverage, following JaCoCo's convention.

Geometric mean of coverage dimensions, used in quality score

(instr% × branch% × line%)^(1/3)

Geometric mean of instruction, branch, and line coverage. Each dimension is clamped to a minimum of 1% before the product is taken. The geometric mean disproportionately punishes weakness in any single dimension: a class with 100% line and instruction but 10% branch scores ≈46% versus 70% under a simple mean. METHOD is excluded because compiler-generated synthetic and bridge methods would distort it.

Method mean instruction coverage (mean_method_cov), used in quality score

(1 / N) × Σ instruction%(methodi)

Unweighted mean of instruction coverage across all N methods. Unlike class-level instruction coverage (where large methods dominate), every method contributes equally regardless of size. A class with one large 200-instruction method at 0% and ten small methods at 100% gives instruction% = 20% but method mean = 91%, exposing the untested large method that the headline number masks.

Δ Instr, display only, not in quality score

mean_method_cov − instruction_pct

Signed difference between the per-method mean and JaCoCo's size-weighted class instruction coverage. A positive value means small methods are better covered than large ones. A negative value means large methods carry the coverage while smaller methods go untested. Near zero means coverage is distributed evenly across method sizes.

Quality score, composite

0.35 × Mean(L,B) + 0.35 × Geo mean + 0.30 × Meth. mean

Computed per class, then rolled up to package and project via complexity-weighted averaging. Each class is weighted by its cyclomatic complexity total, so a package with one complex class at 60% and ten trivial classes at 100% reports closer to 60%, not 96%. Grades: A ≥ 90, B ≥ 80, C ≥ 70, D ≥ 60, F < 60.

The AI interpretation layer

AI interpretations are powered by Groq's llama-3.3-70b-versatile via the Groq API. For each request, the relevant metrics are serialised into a structured block and sent with a system prompt instructing the model to be concise, honest about uncertainty, and grounded only in the data shown.

Three prompt types are used: single-target interpretation (project, package, or class), side-by-side comparison of two targets, and free-form follow-up questions. The exact prompt is always visible via the "Show prompt sent to the model" toggle.
